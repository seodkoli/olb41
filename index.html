About Linen

Linen () is a fabric made from the fibers of the flax plant. Bed linen is really solid and absorbing, and it dries out faster than cotton. Due to these residential properties, bed linen is comfortable to use in heat and is valued for usage in garments. Linen textiles can be made from flax plant fiber, thread, as well as woven and knitted. Linen likewise has various other distinct features, such as its propensity to crease. It takes substantially longer to harvest than a material like cotton, although both are natural fibers. It is also harder to weave than cotton. Bed linen textiles seem several of the oldest on the planet; their background returns many hundreds of years. Colored flax fibers found in a cave in the Caucasus (contemporary Georgia) suggest making use of woven bed linen textiles from wild flax might date back over 30,000 years. Bed linen was utilized in ancient human beings including Mesopotamia and ancient Egypt, and bed linen is mentioned in the Bible. In the 18th century and past, the bed linen sector was essential in the economic climates of numerous countries in Europe along with the American colonies.
